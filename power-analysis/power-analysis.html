Recommendations
1
Request your interval data, then switch to Tariff 12A
Call Ergon on 13 10 46 and ask for 12 months of 30-minute interval data from your smart meter — it's free and shows exactly when you import from the grid. With your 10 kWh battery charged by solar each day, the 4–9 pm peak window is likely already covered, making the off-peak rate very advantageous. The data confirms this before you commit to switching. Estimated saving: $290–$360/year.
Est. saving: $290–$360/yr Call Ergon: 13 10 46
2
Add Tariff 31 if you have electric hot water or a pool
Tariff 31 (Controlled Load) runs nominated appliances overnight at ~13¢/kWh — less than half your current flat rate. If your hot water system or pool pump runs off the grid overnight, this is an easy win. It sits alongside your main tariff and requires an electrician to move the circuit. Typical saving for a Queensland household: $300–$425/year.
Est. saving: $300–$425/yr if applicable Ask a local electrician
3
Investigate the 2025 system outage
The system produced nothing for ~4 months in 2025 (July–October) and was low in June. As new owners this is worth understanding — inverter fault, WiFi/monitoring disconnection, or a commissioning gap during the ownership transition. Check the SolaX portal's Alarm Record tab for fault codes. If it was a hardware issue it may still be under the 5-year inverter warranty (commissioned Feb 2021). The outage cost an estimated ~$800 in avoidable grid electricity.
Outage cost: ~$800 estimated Check Alarm Record in SolaX portal
4
Your 87% self-consumption rate is excellent — protect it
At 8.66¢ feed-in vs 30¢ import, every kWh you export is worth 3.5× less than one you use yourself. The battery is already doing most of the work. To squeeze more value: run the dishwasher, washing machine, and any pool pump during solar hours (roughly 9 am–3 pm). Check the SolaX app's battery operating mode is set to Self Use, not grid export or scheduled mode. Avoid shifting large loads to evening peak hours (4–9 pm) if you switch to Tariff 12A.
Check battery mode in SolaX app → Batteries tab
